Conditional Diffusion for Crystal Synthesis

This research focuses on the Inverse Design Problem: generating inorganic crystal structures that satisfy specific physical property constraints, such as target bandgaps. I examined state-of-the-art diffusion models including MatterGen and CrystalGRW.

Technical Implementation

To achieve high-fidelity generation, I utilized diffusion-based Equivariant GNN decoders (Equiformer, GemNet). These architectures ensure that the model respects the rotational and translational symmetries of crystal lattices.

Conditioning Schemes

  • Adapter-based Conditioning
  • Node-based Feature Injection
  • Skewed-dataset Fine-tuning

Core Architectures

  • Equiformer (Equivariant Transformer)
  • GemNet (Geometric Message Passing)
  • Riemannian Flow Matching

Impact

By implementing these novel conditioning schemes, the models showed up to a 5 times improvement in both generation control and novelty. This allows for the discovery of valid crystals in chemical spaces previously unexplored by standard generative approaches.

Research Specs

  • Category: Generative AI / Material Informatics
  • Frameworks: PyTorch, MatterGen, CrystalGRW
  • GNN Type: Equivariant Graph Neural Networks
  • Focus: Targeted Property Generation (Bandgap)
