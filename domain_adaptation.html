1.5x - 2x Accuracy Improvement

Overcoming Data Scarcity via Transfer Learning

A major challenge in discovering new materials, such as Halide Perovskites, is the limited amount of high-quality annotated data. This project focused on Parameter-based Domain Adaptation to leverage knowledge from large datasets (source domains) to improve performance on specialized subsets (target domains).

Multi-Model Implementation

I implemented and benchmarked these adaptation techniques across three state-of-the-art Graph Neural Network architectures:

SchNet
PAiNN
ALIGNN

Core Methodology

  • Weight Initialization: Utilizing pre-trained weights from single perovskites(ABX3) dataset.
  • Fine-Tuning: Strategically unfreezing layers to adapt to specific chemical spaces double perovskites(AA'BB'X6).
  • Parameter Matching: Ensuring feature consistency across different material distributions.

Research Impact

The results demonstrated a 1.5x to 2x increase in accuracy for property predictions. This improvement is pivotal for virtual screening, as it allows for the rapid and reliable identification of stable new halide perovskites before they are ever synthesized in a lab.
