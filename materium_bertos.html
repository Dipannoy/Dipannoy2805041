Hybrid Generative Framework for Stable Crystal Synthesis

The CrysLLMGen project addresses a critical bottleneck in material informatics: the generation of chemically valid and stable inorganic crystals. Current generative models often struggle with charge neutrality and realistic atomic arrangements.

Technical Core: Oxidation State Integration

My primary contribution involves the integration of BERTOS and TOSS models to predict high-accuracy oxidation states. By embedding these specialized models, we ensure that the generated structures are not just geometrically plausible, but electronically stable.

TOSS Integration BERTOS NLP Materium Backbone Charge Neutrality

By coupling these oxidation-state predictors with the Materium codebase, the framework can filter and refine generated structures in real-time. This pipeline facilitates "Inverse Design," where we start with a desired property and work backward to the atomic coordinates, significantly reducing the time required for lab synthesis.
