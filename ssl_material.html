Evaluating Contrastive Learning for Crystal Encoders

As Self-Supervised Learning (SSL) thrives in Image and Language domains, its application to 3D Crystal Structures requires rigorous benchmarking. This project involved training Equivariant GNN encoders using contrastive learning strategies over massive unlabelled datasets.

214,000

Materials Project Structures

The Challenge

The primary objective was to determine if pre-training on 360,000+ unlabelled structures could provide a superior starting point for downstream property prediction tasks compared to standard supervised training from scratch.

Research Finding

Extensive downstream evaluations revealed limited gains for pre-trained SSL models over supervised baselines in this specific domain. This critical result helps define the boundaries of where SSL is—and isn't—effective for materials modeling, specifically highlighting the importance of labels in high-symmetry crystal systems.

Key Research Contributions

  • Implementation of Contrastive Learning objectives for 3D equivariant representations.
  • Scaling GNN training across high-performance computing (HPC) nodes for massive datasets.
  • Rigorous benchmarking across multiple downstream tasks (formation energy, bandgap, etc.).
